üî¨ Portfolio as Systemic Exploration

My portfolio centers on interactive works that explore how player experience is shaped through movement, feedback, and systemic design. While games are my primary medium, I use them to study how rules and algorithms translate into perception, emotion, and meaning.

2D Platformer Adventure

Despite its visual simplicity, this project functions as a study of embodied interaction and game feel. Movement physics, camera behavior, enemy behavior, and combat rules were treated as interconnected systems that shape player expression.

I focused on how players learn through interaction rather than instruction. Level design, enemy placement, and feedback were structured to guide understanding organically, allowing curiosity and experimentation to drive engagement. This project reflects my belief that meaning in interactive media emerges through doing rather than explanation.

Machine Learning Connect 4

In this project, I explored how different AI decision-making systems affect player psychology and emotional response. By comparing Minimax-based AI with a NEAT-trained neural network, I studied how optimal versus imperfect systems change perceived fairness, tension, and agency.

Rather than treating AI as a purely technical system, I approached it as a creative agent within the game. This allowed me to see machine learning as a medium that shapes experience rather than a background algorithm.

Procedural Generation Maze

This project examines how procedural systems and spatial logic influence navigation and spatial understanding. Through algorithmic maze generation, I explored how randomness, repetition, and variation affect players' ability to form mental maps and make decisions under uncertainty.

By observing player behavior, I reflected on how abstract computational structures translate into lived spatial experience.
